Education

Education often times is as important as recycling itself. Our main target audience; people who care about the environment, recycle for the purpose of sustainability and others who are visiting the city or want to participate out of curiosity should be educated on what they are consuming and how they are affecting the environment.

Understanding why and how we are recycling, cleaning, and reusing provides transparency and educates people on our base values and target goals. Providing statistics on how much less waste we are creating in comparison to the numbers provided by the city are all part of providing people with the knowledge on how we are impacting the environment. Part of the reason water fountains, water filtration systems and NYC tap water is not trusted is the lack of knowledge on the chemical components in the water. Another aspect of our transparent goal is by providing people with DEP safe drinking water requirements in comparison to our quarterly water testing results. Bottled water based on FDA regulations is not tested as often as tap water, with this in mind our main goal is to continuously test the water we are providing to the people. The easiest form of education is by having pictures and posters at locations in the city with the most foot traffic, bus stops and subway stations. For the population who would like to do further research, they can find more information on our website and video of how it all came to be.
